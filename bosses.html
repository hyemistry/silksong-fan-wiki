Bosses

Act 1

Moss Mother

Theme:
"Strive" by Christopher Larkin

Tutorial boss; the mother of all creatures (and also the largest) in Moss Grotto. Once the Needolin is acquired, two Moss Mothers can be fought in the Weavenest Atla.

If the player reaches Act 3, they can fight a void-possessed version of her in Moss Grotto.

Bell Beast

Theme:
"Bell Beast" by Christopher Larkin

A beast first found stuck in the silk threads of The Marrow. At first, she is hostile—once Hornet knocks some sense into her, she becomes both a companion and the counterpart of Hollow Knight’s transportation Stags.

Lace

Theme:
"Lace" by Christopher Larkin

A being made out of Grand Mother Silk’s threads who yearned for her attention. However, as she thought Grand Mother Silk preferred Hornet, she fights her in the Deep Docks in her first attempt to stop her.

Fourth Chorus

Theme:
"Fourth Chorus" by Christopher Larkin

A robot that targets the last of The Order of the Pinstress’ still alive warriors, which in turn mistakes Hornet for one after receiving the Drifter's Cloak.

Moorwing

Theme:
"Strive" by Christopher Larkin

A vicious bird-like bug, Moorwing tends to feast on pilgrims to pass on Bellhart. The editor, in fact, died more times to this boss than she did on Grand Mother Silk.

Moorwing's fight can be skipped if the "The Lost Fleas" wish had been granted, which makes the Flea Caravan move to its arena. If this happens, Moorwing can instead be fought in Act 2 right next to the Greymoor Bellway.

Sister Splinter

Theme:
"Sister Splinter" by Christopher Larkin

After the demise of their former protector, Sister Splinter and her kin conquered Shellwood.

Defeating her grants Hornet access to the Cling Grip, a tool used to cling to walls, much like the Mantis Claw in the original Hollow Knight.

Widow

Theme:
"Widow" by Christopher Larkin

A Weaver who had her mask taken and has two pins lodged through her spine, rendering her silk useless. She is helping Grand Mother Silk bring about the Haunting to Bellhart.

Defeating her grants the player the Needolin, which allows Hornet to play songs in her needle with her silk. Her defeat also brings back Bellhart to its original form.

Last Judge

Theme:
"Last Judge" by Christopher Larkin

The Judge guarding the gates to the Citadel. She lets all pilgrims without darkness in their hearts pass through.

Her fight can be skipped by fighting Phantom instead to go to the Citadel, and can be instead fought in Act 2.

Phantom

Theme:
"Phantom" by Christopher Larkin

Grand Mother Silk’s second child who was discarded and was tasked to maintain the Exhaust Organ in Bilewater. She can be reached by traversing The Mist through Sinner's Road or Bilewater.

Fighting her in Act 1 instead of the Last Judge will open an alternative route to the Citadel.

Act 2

Cogwork Dancers

Theme:
"Cogwork Dancers" by Christopher Larkin

The last defense before opening the routes to The Cradle, these majestic dancers are molded from the shell and host the soul of the Green Prince’s lover in his last attempt to protect Verdania.

Trobbio

Theme:
"Trobbio" by Christopher Larkin

A bug that avoided the Haunting, Trobbio seeks the attention of all of Pharloom through his flamboyant performances.

Disgraced Chef Lugoli

Theme:
"Cut Through" by Christopher Larkin

She used to work in the Citadel, but due to her culinary atrocities, she was exiled and finally settled in Sinner's Road.

To fight her, the player must promise the "Great Taste of Pharloom" wish of Loyal Mergwin. Defeating her gives Hornet the Pickled Muckmaggot.

Broodmother

Theme:
"Cut Through" by Christopher Larkin

The mad matriach of The Slab, in which all bugs in that area came from.

To fight her, the player must promise the "The Wailing Mother" wish in Songclave.

First Sinner

Theme:
"Widow" by Christopher Larkin

A Weaver who was imprisoned in The Slab due to her sin of apostasy.

Defeating her grants Hornet the silk skill Rune Rage.

Second Sentinel

Theme:
"The Choir" by Christopher Larkin

An automaton robot originally built to protect the pilgrims of the Citadel. The Second Sentinel can be fought by assembling the Cogheart and by promising the "Final Audience" wish in Songclave.

Defeating them grants Hornet the tool Reserve Bind.

Groal the Great

Theme:
"Cut Through" by Christopher Larkin

They were the head of the Stilkin Tribe who opposed Grand Mother Silk’s Haunting, but eventually, them and their tribe also became haunted by her threads.

Defeating him grants Hornet the Seeker's Soul, an important piece to grant the "Silk and Soul" wish by the Caretaker in Songclave.

Forebrothers Signis & Gron

Theme:
"Fourth Chorus" by Christopher Larkin

They are the overseers of the Deep Docks. Fighting them grants Hornet the access to the Diving Bell, which is crucial to progress in Act 3.

Shakra

Singing

Theme:
"Generic Enemy Battle A Track" by Christopher Larkin

A wandering cartographer mapping out areas around Pharloom in pursuit to her long lost master.

Dueling with her can only be unlocked if all of her maps were bought and if her wish, Trail's End, was granted.

Garmond and Zaza

Theme:
"Generic Enemy Battle A Track" by Christopher Larkin

Two bugs that help you in the Moorwing fight if you so asked. After you visit Lower Songclave twice, they will ask you to spar with them. This is the only time you can fight them.

Lace

Theme:
"Lace" by Christopher Larkin

The second fight with Lace in an attempt to stop Hornet from ascending to The Cradle.

Grand Mother Silk

Theme:
"Awakening" by Christopher Larkin

Lace and Phantom's mother, this pale being resides in the Cradle due to the Weaver's slow betrayal of making her asleep for eternity. However, at some point in time, she began the Haunting of Pharloom.

Defeating her in different circumstances grant different endings:
* Ending 1: Defeat her normally and get the Weaver Queen ending.
* Ending 2: Defeat her while cursed by the Twisted Bud and get the Twisted Child ending.
* Ending 3: Granting almost all wishes across Pharloom and entrapping her with the Soul Snare through the Needolin will get you the Snared Silk ending, which in turn grants the player access to Act 3.

Act 3

Shrine Guardian Seth

Theme:
"Nyleth" by Christopher Larkin

The eternal defender of the shrine of Nyleth, the heart of Shellwood.

Defeating him grants Hornet access to Nyleth's shrine.

Nyleth

Theme:
"Nyleth" by Christopher Larkin

The heart of Shellwood, she granted the area with the vegetation Pharloom recognizes today.

To fight her, Hornet must play the Elegy of the Deep. Defeating her grants Hornet the Pollen Heart.

Crust King Khann

Theme:
"Crust King Khann" by Christopher Larkin

He was the former ruler of the Karak, who was the last to resist Grand Mother Silk's rule. After the Karak dried up to become deserted, he and the last of his warriors died in the Coral Tower.

To fight him, Hornet must play the Elegy of the Deep. Defeating him grants Hornet the Encrusted Heart.

Skarrsinger Karmelita

Theme:
"Skarrsinger Karmelita" by Christopher Larkin

She was once the strongest in her tribe at the Hunter's March, who used her own power as their song queen to resist the Haunting. Hornet describes her as a match to her own skills.

To fight her, Hornet must play the Elegy of the Deep. Defeating her grants Hornet the Hunter's Heart.

Clover Dancers

Theme:
"Clover Dancers" by Christopher Larkin

The Green Prince and his lover, who was the mold of the Cogwork Dancers, finally reunited in the memories of Lost Verdania.

To fight them, Hornet must play the Elegy of the Deep. Defeating them grants Hornet the Conjoined Heart, which can be used as an alternative to The Old Hearts quest. It can also be put up in Hornet's Bellhome.

Lost Lace

Theme:
"Lost Lace" by Christopher Larkin

The true final boss of Hollow Knight: Silksong, Lost Lace is a void-possessed version of Lace who was dragged down to the Abyss with Grand Mother Silk in an attempt to free Hornet.

Defeating her grants the player the Sister of the Void ending.
